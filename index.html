The Problem.

The $160 Billion Waste

American consumers discard approximately 133 billion pounds of food annually. For the average family of four, this translates to $1,500 - $2,000 in lost capital every year simply due to "expiration anxiety"â€”the fear that food has spoiled when it is actually still safe.

Current storage solutions (Tupperware, Ziploc) are passive. They hold food but do not monitor it. Fresh-Check makes storage active.

The Odorless Threat

Biological pathogens like Listeria and Salmonella do not always produce a scent during the early stages of growth. Relying on the "Sniff Test" is a dangerous health gamble for families.

Plastic Dependency

Single-use cling wraps are a leading cause of microplastic ocean pollution and fail to create a high-tension vacuum seal, which actually accelerates food oxidation.
